The Economics of Global Supply Chains

Examining the factors that drive, disrupt, and define modern international trade.

1. Key Drivers of Global Supply Chains

Globalization and technological advancements have made it possible for companies to break down the production process and source components from around the world to maximize efficiency and minimize costs.

Core Economic Concepts

  • **Comparative Advantage:** The primary reason for specialization and trade between countries.
  • **Economies of Scale:** Lower costs achieved by increasing production volume.
  • **Just-in-Time (JIT) Inventory:** A strategy to increase efficiency and reduce waste by receiving goods only as they are needed.

3. Understanding Disruption and Risk

Recent events like geopolitical conflicts and the COVID-19 pandemic have highlighted the fragility of overly optimized supply chains, leading many firms to consider **reshoring** or **diversification**.
